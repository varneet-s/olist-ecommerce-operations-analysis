# Business Recommendations

## Context

These recommendations are based on analysis of Olist's 2016–2018 order, delivery, seller,
and customer data. Each recommendation targets a specific gap identified in the analysis
and is grounded in the dataset. Olist operates as a marketplace and commerce enabler —
all recommendations stay within that operating model.

---

## Recommendation 1 — Logistics Partnerships in Northern States

**Addresses:** H4 — Freight costs in northern states exceeding 50% of product price

**Rationale:**
Customers in northern states are still placing orders despite paying freight costs that
can exceed half the product price. That is not a market with no demand — it is a market
with a logistics cost problem. Regional carrier partnerships, or an extension of Olist
Envios coverage into the north would bring freight costs to a level where northern
orders become economically viable for both sellers and customers at scale.

**Owner:** Operations & Logistics Team

**Priority:** High

**Effort:** Medium — requires carrier negotiation and route coverage expansion

---

## Recommendation 2 — Reduce Transit Time in Remote Routes

**Addresses:** H1 — Late deliveries averaging 2.27 review score vs 4.03 on-time

**Rationale:**
Late deliveries are not a minor inconvenience — they are a platform credibility problem.
A 1.76-point review score gap between late and on-time orders is large enough to affect
seller rankings, repeat purchase rates, and customer trust. The root cause is not seller
behaviour — it is route complexity. Northern states have longer distances, fewer carrier
options, and infrastructure constraints the fulfilment workflow was never designed to
handle. Reducing transit time requires carrier-level intervention: faster regional routes,
better carrier selection for remote destinations, or hub-based dispatch closer to
underserved regions.

**Owner:** Operations & Logistics Team

**Priority:** High

**Effort:** High — requires infrastructure changes and carrier SLA renegotiation

---

## Recommendation 3 — Seller Acquisition in Underserved States

**Addresses:** H2 — SP generating 42% of revenue, top 3 states generating ~70%

**Rationale:**
When 42% of revenue depends on one state, any disruption to that market —
regulatory, economic, or competitive — disproportionately affects the platform.
A targeted seller acquisition programme in tier 2 and tier 3 states would reduce
concentration risk, shorten the distance between sellers and northern customers
(directly improving freight costs and transit times), and open a growth channel
that competitors have not yet saturated.

**Owner:** Sales & Marketing Team

**Priority:** Medium — most effective after logistics barriers are reduced. Sellers
are unlikely to join a platform where their customers face 50-60% freight costs.

**Effort:** Medium — requires campaign design, incentive structure, and onboarding support

---

## Recommendation 4 — Introduce Seller Tiering Based on GMV Contribution

**Addresses:** H3 — Top 20% of sellers generating 82.54% of GMV

**Rationale:**
592 sellers are currently generating the majority of platform revenue with no
differentiated treatment. If a meaningful portion of them churn or reduce activity,
the impact on GMV is immediate. Segmenting sellers into tiers — high-GMV and standard
— and assigning dedicated support or account management to the top tier protects
the revenue base. Retention is cheaper than replacement.

**Owner:** Seller Success Team

**Priority:** High

**Effort:** Low — requires policy change and CRM segmentation, not new infrastructure

---

## Prioritisation Summary (MoSCoW)

| Recommendation | Priority | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Logistics partnerships in northern states | Must Have | Directly removes highest-cost barrier for northern customers |
| Reduce transit time in remote routes | Must Have | Flows from logistics infrastructure — addresses retention risk |
| Seller tiering — protect top 20% | Must Have | Low effort, high impact, protects existing revenue base |
| Seller acquisition in underserved states | Should Have | Most effective after logistics barriers are reduced |

---

## What This Analysis Does Not Recommend

- Building warehouses or shifting to a stock-holding model — outside Olist's operating model
- Abandoning the SP market — concentration is a risk, not a reason to pull back from a
  high-performing region
- Category-level changes — category analysis was out of scope for this project
